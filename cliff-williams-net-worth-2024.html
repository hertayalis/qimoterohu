Early Life and Career Beginnings

Cliff Williams was born in Romford, Essex, England, and began his musical journey at a young age. His passion for music quickly turned into a professional pursuit, and by the early 1970s, he was playing for various bands. Williams’ early career set the foundation for his later success with AC/DC.

In 1977, Williams joined AC/DC, a move that would catapult him into the limelight and significantly impact his net worth. With the band, he contributed to some of the most iconic albums in rock history, including “Back in Black” and “Highway to Hell.”
